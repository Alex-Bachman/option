A Sampler of Classic Metal

Starting in the late 1960s, bands had begun looking for a heavier sound, more aggressive vocals and instrumentation, harsh distortion on the guitars, fast paced drumming and either harsh ( screamed or growled) or clean (sung normally) vocals, although the harsher vocals would came later in the genres development.

Black Sabbath is often credited as one of the very first metal bands, their sound would go on to influence generations of musicians even to this day, alongside Deep Purple and Led Zeppelin, these three bands would ignite the spark that makes metal what it is today. When many people think of metal, they often think of screaming and noise, while that may be true for some people, I think that this genre carries a lot of value in its history and influences, as well as being enjoyable to listen to. In terms of genres that influenced bands, we look to the 60s, with psych rock, jazz, and blues rock being significant contributors to the traditional heavy metal sound.

Jazz and metal songs often share similar song structures. Although the genres have evolved over the years, there is still strong similarities, mainly in the musical arrangements in terms of an ensemble. Melodic instruments such as guitar and piano are backed up by a rhythm section consisting of bass and drums, often times with a vocalist though often times without!
